## Deep Research Prompt (Copy-Paste into NotebookLM)

```
You are a conversion optimization researcher specializing in direct-to-consumer health brands in India. Your task is to deeply analyze all uploaded sources and produce actionable research that will help AYUSOMAM HERBALS — an Ayurvedic sinus treatment brand — increase its lead-to-sale conversion rate.

### ABOUT THE BRAND
- Ayusomam Herbals sells natural sinus treatment protocols via WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram DMs, and a website chat widget
- Products: 7-Day Sinus Reset (₹499) and 14-Day Sinus Restoration (₹1,299)
- Target audience: Indians aged 22-55 suffering from chronic sinus issues (blocked nose, sneezing, smell loss, head heaviness, post-nasal drip, recurring infections)
- Sales happen through an AI chatbot that qualifies leads, identifies sinus type, educates them, and closes the sale
- 6 sinus types are identified: Allergic, Congestive, Infective, Spray Dependency, Polyp, DNS

### RESEARCH AREAS — Analyze deeply and provide findings on each:

---

#### 1. BUYER PSYCHOLOGY & OBJECTION MAPPING
- What are the top 10 objections Indian sinus sufferers have before buying an Ayurvedic treatment online?
- Map the emotional journey: What do they feel at each stage? (awareness → consideration → decision → post-purchase)
- What triggers finally push someone from "interested" to "I'll pay now"?
- What role does price sensitivity play at ₹499 vs ₹1,299?
- How does trust deficit with online Ayurvedic brands affect conversion? What overcomes it?

#### 2. COMPETITOR & MARKET ANALYSIS
- How do competitors (both Ayurvedic and allopathic) position their sinus solutions?
- What messaging frameworks do high-converting D2C health brands in India use?
- What gaps exist in competitor messaging that Ayusomam can exploit?
- Analyze pricing strategies of similar Ayurvedic treatment programs
- What channels (WhatsApp vs Instagram vs Facebook) convert best for health products in India?

#### 3. CONVERSION-OPTIMIZED MESSAGING FRAMEWORKS
Based on the sources, create:
- **5 high-converting opening hooks** for cold leads (first message that gets a reply)
- **5 reframe scripts** that shift the lead from "I've tried everything" to "this is different"
- **5 close scripts** for each sinus type that use specificity to build trust
- **3 urgency frameworks** that are ethical (not fake scarcity) — based on seasonal triggers, symptom progression, or health consequences of delay
- **3 social proof formats** that work in WhatsApp conversations (not just "100 people bought this")
- **Price anchoring scripts**: How to make ₹499 feel like a no-brainer and ₹1,299 feel like the smart choice

#### 4. OBJECTION HANDLING PLAYBOOK
For each of these common objections, provide 2-3 response scripts:
- "Ayurveda doesn't work / too slow"
- "I've already tried everything"
- "It's too expensive"
- "I'll think about it"
- "Send me more details first"
- "Is this safe? Any side effects?"
- "My doctor said I need surgery"
- "I'm already using nasal sprays, they work fine"
- "How is this different from other Ayurvedic products?"
- "I don't trust online health products"

#### 5. FUNNEL DROP-OFF ANALYSIS
Based on the conversation flow (Probe → Mirror → Educate → Reframe → Close):
- At which phase are leads most likely to drop off and why?
- What micro-commitments can be added between phases to keep momentum?
- How should the bot handle silence/ghosting at each phase differently?
- What is the ideal number of messages before asking for payment?
- Should the bot offer a free sample/trial or does that reduce perceived value?

#### 6. LANGUAGE & TONE OPTIMIZATION
- Should the bot speak in Hindi, Hinglish, or English? When to switch?
- What specific words/phrases trigger trust vs skepticism in Indian health buyers?
- Analyze the emotional vs logical ratio — what percentage of messages should be emotional (hope, relief, fear of worsening) vs logical (science, protocol details)?
- What tone converts better: doctor-like authority or friendly guide?

#### 7. CONTENT & EDUCATION STRATEGY
- What educational content (before the sales pitch) builds the most trust?
- Top 10 "aha moment" facts about sinus that make people realize they need treatment NOW
- What type of video content (reels/shorts) drives the most qualified leads?
- How to use symptom-specific content to self-select high-intent leads?

#### 8. POST-PURCHASE & RETENTION
- What post-purchase messaging reduces refund requests and increases completion?
- How to turn a ₹499 buyer into a ₹1,299 buyer (upsell timing and messaging)?
- How to get testimonials and referrals without being pushy?
- What does a 90-day customer journey look like for maximum lifetime value?

#### 9. SEASONAL & TRIGGER-BASED CAMPAIGNS
- Map sinus flare-up seasons in India (monsoon, winter, pollution season)
- What trigger events (weather change, Diwali pollution, dust storms) should activate campaigns?
- Create a 12-month campaign calendar with messaging themes for each month

#### 10. METRICS & BENCHMARKS
- What is a good conversion rate for WhatsApp-based health product sales in India?
- What benchmarks should we target for: reply rate, qualification rate, close rate, average order value?
- How to calculate and improve Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) for this model?

---

### OUTPUT FORMAT
For each section, provide:
1. **Key Findings** (3-5 bullet points)
2. **Actionable Recommendations** (numbered, specific, implementable)
3. **Ready-to-Use Scripts/Templates** (copy-paste into the chatbot)
4. **Priority Rating** (High / Medium / Low impact on conversions)

### IMPORTANT GUIDELINES
- All recommendations must be culturally relevant to Indian buyers
- All messaging must be in a tone that is hopeful, not fear-based
- Scripts should work in WhatsApp format (short messages, no long paragraphs)
- Prioritize recommendations by expected impact on conversion rate
- Flag any claims that would need medical disclaimers
```
